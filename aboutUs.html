About Us

Since 2015, our 501C(3) nonprofit has been preparing young professionals in Greater Cincinnati for its diverse and inclusive workforce. One that will ensure equitable, sustainable economic growth.

We achieve our mission through professional development, training, job placement, and opportunities to lead, mentor & network.

Our Mission

To provide world-class networking, mentorship, career training, and resources to help young professionals succeed.

Our Ideals

We value the inclusivity of diverse people regardless of the background or experience. Transformation through professional development, and in our community. Putting people first in all we do.

Our Purpose

What is going on in Cincinnati?

  • Unemployment

    • The unemployment rate in Cincinnati has dropped each year since 2016 (except the COVID-related hike). We need to keep it down. We must also close the racial gap, e.g., the unemployment rate between black people is twice that among whites.

  • Diversity, Equity, Inclusion

    • Diversity is a driver of economic growth.A recent study found that Black-owned businesses in Cincinnati contribute over $2.1 billion to the local economy. Yet, Cincinnati ranks 17th out of 22, on Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DEI) indicators. Our relationship lacks diversity too. Cincinnati ranks 19th of 22 in C-suite racial diversity and 26th of 22 in C-suite gender diversity. Our area is becoming ever more diverse. We are looking for trouble if we leave it horribly unequal.

  • Conductive for Youth

    • Forbes ranked Cincinnati the best place for young professionals to live, out of 100 metro areas considered. Cincinnati ranks 6th on median rent as a percentage of individual income and 7th on median home costs as a percentage pf household income. We must keep our young here and attract the best talent.

  • For Future Leaders of Cincinnati

    • Greater Cincinnati needs strong, united leaders. GenNOW's equips young professionals with the connections and knowhow they need to become fine leaders. We provide them with resources they need to success, regardless of beackground or experience. And we bring them together as a diverse yet inclusive community.

Our Story

Nine years ago, GenNOW Cincinnati was stated by young professionals for young professionals. Here's how we have grown:

2015

Founder & CEO Renika J. Smiley starts Empower GenNOW Cincinnati network. 4-member board identifies young professionals' challenges and hosts Happy Hour events, at rented facilities, to talk heart-to-heart with local youth. Events average 50 attendees. Discussions lead to:

  • Network of 1,000+ young professionals
  • 8 programs for professional development
  • $15,000 for funding those programs
  • Launch of Top 10 List awards to recognize young professionals for professional excellence and public service 100 awardees.

2016

2,000+ young professionals.
We collaborate with local businesses to host events at their workplaces instead of rented facilities; thus, we cut costs.
We add:

  • 4 Board Members
  • 2 committees to guide us on professional development programs and executive initiatives.
  • 10 volunteers
  • Mayor John Cranley publicly congratulates us for our Believe Youth Conference. 75 attend this conference for young professionals.

2017

3,000+ young professionals.
We enforce internal resources, recruit new sponsors, and add workshops.
Key achievements:

  • $25,000+ in sponsorships
  • New sponsors include P&G, 84.51, and Kroger`s.
  • New programs, workshops, and social events such as Cross-Cultural Communication, Conflict Management, Networking, Leadership Development, and Diversity & Inclusion.
  • Workshops average 100 participants.
